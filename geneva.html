Mont-Blance

Mont Blanc, meaning “White Mountain,” is the highest peak in the Alps and Western Europe, standing at 4,809 meters (15,777 feet) above sea level. Located on the border between France and Italy, near the Swiss frontier, it is a popular destination for mountaineers, hikers, and nature lovers. The mountain is part of the Mont Blanc Massif and is renowned for its stunning snow-covered peaks, glaciers, and breathtaking alpine scenery. Chamonix in France and Courmayeur in Italy are the main gateway towns, offering access to various outdoor activities year-round. Whether for skiing, climbing, or simply admiring its majestic beauty, Mont Blanc remains one of Europe's most iconic natural landmarks.

CERN Science Gate

CERN Science Gateway is a place to explore CERN and science through authentic, innovative and inspirational experiences. Through immersive multimedia exhibits, hands-on lab workshops, science shows, events that blend science and culture, innovation-prototyping workshops and tours of authentic CERN places all guided by CERN people visitors of all ages and backgrounds can engage in the discoveries, the science and the technologies of CERN.

Patek Phillipe Museum

The Patek Philippe Museum in Geneva is considered one of the world's foremost horology museums. It houses over five centuries of watchmaking history into two important collections: the extraordinary antique collection starting from the 16th century, which includes the earliest watch ever made and the Patek Philippe collection from 1839 onwards.
