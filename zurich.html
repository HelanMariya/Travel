High lights

Grossmunster

The Grossmünster is one of Zurich's most iconic landmarks, known for its impressive twin towers and rich historical significance. Built in Romanesque style around the 12th century, it played a central role in the Swiss Reformation led by Huldrych Zwingli. According to legend, the church stands on the graves of Zurich's patron saints, Felix and Regula. Visitors can admire its stunning architecture, including modern stained-glass windows by renowned artists, and climb the towers for breathtaking views of the city and Lake Zurich. Located along the Limmat River, the Grossmünster remains a must-visit site that beautifully blends Zurich's medieval roots with its cultural evolution.

Lindenof Hill

Lindenhof Hill is a peaceful and historic spot located in the heart of Zurich, offering panoramic views of the Old Town, the Limmat River, and the city’s famous churches. Once the site of a Roman fort and later a medieval castle, it is now a tranquil park surrounded by lime trees, charming pathways, and benches. The hill is a favorite among locals and tourists alike for relaxing walks, scenic photo opportunities, and a glimpse into Zurich's ancient past. With its elevated position and serene atmosphere, Lindenhof Hill provides a perfect escape from the city's hustle while still being just a short walk from major attractions.

Bahnhofstrasse

Bahnhofstrasse is one of the world’s most famous shopping streets, located in the heart of Zurich, Switzerland. Stretching from Zurich’s main train station (Bahnhof) to Lake Zurich, this elegant boulevard is lined with luxury boutiques, international fashion brands, Swiss watchmakers, and upscale department stores. More than just a shopping destination, Bahnhofstrasse offers a vibrant atmosphere with beautifully maintained streets, historic buildings, and cozy cafés. It is also home to Paradeplatz, a major financial hub and the center of Zurich’s banking industry. Whether you're window shopping or indulging in high-end retail, a stroll down Bahnhofstrasse is a must for anyone visiting Zurich.
